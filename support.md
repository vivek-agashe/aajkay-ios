# Aaj Kay? - Support

**Preserve and share your family recipes**

"Aaj Kay?" is a recipe management app designed to help you organize, preserve, and share your favorite recipes, especially traditional Indian and Marathi dishes. Whether you're cooking solo or sharing recipes with your family, aajkay makes it easy to keep your culinary traditions alive.

## Features

- **Organize Your Recipes** - Sort recipes into categories (Snacks, Curries, Desserts, etc.)
- **Detailed Recipe Cards** - Track serving sizes, ingredients, proportions, cooking instructions, and notes
- **Traditional Ingredients** - Dedicated sections for Fodni (tempering), Sourness, and Garnish with common Marathi ingredients
- **Family Sharing** - Create a family collection where everyone can contribute and sync recipes
- **Import & Export** - Easily share recipes with friends or back up your collection
- **Personal or Family Mode** - Use individually or share a unified family recipe collection

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Getting Started

**Q: How do I set up my profile?**  
When you first open aajkay, you'll be prompted to enter your name. You can also choose to create or join a family collection for shared recipes.

**Q: What's the difference between single mode and family mode?**  
- **Single Mode:** Your recipes are private and stored as "My Recipes"
- **Family Mode:** All family members contribute to a shared "Family Recipes" collection that syncs across devices

### Creating Recipes

**Q: How do I add a new recipe?**  
Tap the **New Recipe** button at the bottom. Fill in the recipe details including title, category, ingredients, instructions, and more. Don't forget to use the Fodni, Sourness, and Garnish checklists for traditional ingredients!

**Q: What is "Fodni"?**  
Fodni (also called tadka or tempering) is a cooking technique used in Indian cuisine where spices are briefly fried in oil or ghee. The app includes a checklist of common fodni ingredients like Tel (oil), Jeera (cumin), Halad (turmeric), and more.

**Q: Can I add custom ingredients to the checklists?**  
Yes! The Fodni section allows you to add custom ingredients. There's also an "additional ingredients" field for each section.

### Categories

**Q: How do I organize recipes into categories?**  
When creating or editing a recipe, select a category from the dropdown menu. You can also create, rename, reorder, and delete categories by tapping the edit button in the main view.

**Q: What categories are included by default?**  
The app comes with common categories like Snacks, Curries, Breads, Desserts, and Other. You can customize these to fit your needs.

### Family Sharing

**Q: How do I share recipes with my family?**  
1. Set up your family profile in Settings
2. Share your family code with family members
3. Family members can import using the code
4. Everyone's recipes sync into the shared "Family Recipes" collection

**Q: What happens if two family members edit the same recipe?**  
The app uses timestamp-based conflict resolution - the newest version wins. You can see who last edited each recipe in the recipe details.

**Q: Can I see who created or edited a recipe?**  
Yes! Each recipe shows who last edited it, which is especially useful in family mode.

### Importing & Exporting

**Q: How do I export my recipes?**  
Tap the **export** button (share icon). You can export all your recipes or select specific categories. The file can be shared via Messages, Mail, AirDrop, or saved to Files.

**Q: How do I import recipes?**  
Tap the **import** button and select a recipe file. The app will automatically detect if it's from you, a family member, or someone else.

**Q: What happens when I import recipes?**  
- **Your own recipes (self-import):** Duplicates are skipped
- **Family recipes:** Newer versions update existing recipes
- **External recipes:** You can choose to import as your own or keep as a separate shared collection

**Q: What file format does aajkay use?**  
Recipes are exported as `.json` files in JSON format, making them easy to share and back up.

### Favorites & Editing

**Q: How do I mark a recipe as favorite?**  
While viewing a recipe, tap the star icon to mark it as a favorite. Favorite recipes appear at the top of their category.

**Q: Can I edit recipes from shared collections?**  
Family recipes can be edited by any family member. Recipes from external shared collections are view-only, but you can import them as your own to make edits.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: My import isn't working. What should I do?**  
Make sure you're selecting a valid `.json` file. If the error persists, try:
1. Re-exporting the recipes from the source device
2. Checking that you have the latest version of the app
3. Contacting support with the error message

**Q: I don't see my family member's recipes.**  
Make sure:
1. You're both using the same family ID
2. You've imported their exported recipe file
3. You're in family mode (check your profile settings)

**Q: Can I delete a recipe?**  
Yes, swipe left on any recipe in the list view and tap the delete button. This can't be undone, so make sure to export important recipes first!

## Privacy & Data

Your privacy matters to us. "Aaj Kay?" stores all your recipe data locally on your device using Apple's secure SwiftData framework. 

**We do NOT:**
- Collect any personal information
- Upload your recipes to any server
- Track your usage or behavior
- Share your data with third parties

**Recipe sharing:** When you export recipes to share with family or friends, you control exactly what is shared and with whom. All sharing happens through standard iOS methods (Messages, AirDrop, Mail, etc.).

## Tips & Best Practices

### Recipe Organization
- Use consistent category names across family members for better organization
- Add detailed notes for special tips or family variations
- Include who the recipe came from in the notes (e.g., "Grandma's version")

### Family Sharing
- Export and share recipes regularly to keep everyone's collections in sync
- Use descriptive recipe titles so family members can easily find them
- Add proportions and serving sizes to help with meal planning

### Backup Your Recipes
- Regularly export your entire collection and save it to iCloud Drive or another backup location
- Consider sharing exports with family members as an additional backup

## About the Name

**aajkay** (आज काय? / "aaj kay") means "what today" or "what for today" in Marathi. The name reflects the daily question in every household: "What are we cooking today?" - connecting the recipes you've preserved with the meals you're planning today.
